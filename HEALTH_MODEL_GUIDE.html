Health Model Guide

Health is not just whether the process is running

Your model should explain what the runtime knows, what it does not know, what the operator sees, and which actions are allowed when truth is stale or incomplete. Implement evidence for the states your local slice exercises; document other states only as planned follow-up work.

Process health

Is the service alive, serving requests, and within basic resource limits? This is necessary, but it is not enough for a critical runtime.

Operational eligibility

Is the runtime allowed to perform unsafe work right now? This depends on data freshness, dependency state, reconciliation, operator pauses, and rollout state.

Strong signal: your implementation can be alive but not eligible for unsafe actions, and that distinction is visible to operators for the states exercised by your slice.

What the state table should answer

Column Question to answer Do not turn this into
Entry signals What concrete observation moves the runtime into this state? A vague label such as "bad dependency" without a measurable signal.
Operator view What status, timestamp, and affected runtime identity should be visible? A hidden log-only condition that operators cannot see.
Unsafe actions Can new risky work proceed, or should it be blocked? A process-health answer that ignores stale or unknown truth.
Safe actions Can operators pause, inspect, recover, or perform safe management actions? A full stop that blocks recovery operations without explanation.
Alert route Does this page immediately, create a ticket, or only annotate the UI? Paging for every state transition, or paging for none of them.
Runbook What is the first action and what evidence closes the issue? "Restart it" as the whole recovery plan.

Design questions to answer in your own system

Stale data

  • How old can data be before a runtime is degraded or blocked?
  • How do you prove the process is alive but not receiving fresh data?
  • What changes in the operator view when only a subset is affected?

Dependency failure

  • Which dependency failures are page-worthy versus ticket-worthy?
  • What actions remain safe while the dependency is disconnected?
  • What evidence proves recovery is complete?

Reconciliation

  • How do you detect state mismatch after restart or dependency recovery?
  • What is blocked until reconciliation passes?
  • Who can override a blocked state, if anyone?

Operator pause

  • How is a pause different from a crash or incident?
  • What is audited when pause or resume is triggered?
  • What should happen after a bad rollout is paused?

Common pitfalls

  • Equating container liveness with a healthy runtime.
  • Defining states that do not change any operator behavior.
  • Leaving alert severity out of the model.
  • Auto-resuming unsafe work after restart without evidence.
  • Documenting a state that the implementation cannot display or test.
  • Mixing implemented health-state evidence with planned states without labels.
